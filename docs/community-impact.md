# ID Atlas — Community Coaching Impact

A collection of organic mentorship and coaching outcomes that happened outside any paid ID Atlas engagement. These are unpaid Reddit threads, DMs, and community interactions where Mike's advice produced documented career or professional outcomes for the recipient. They are not paying-client testimonials but they are evidence that ID Atlas's community-service framing of Field Notes and Reddit participation produces real impact.

Useful as supporting context for "about us" copy, future blog posts about Reddit mentorship as a service philosophy, and as live examples of the agency's ethos.

## meltyhoney_ (Reddit, dream-job placement)

**Channel:** Reddit thread (r/instructionaldesign) followed by direct messages
**Period:** February 2025 (initial coaching) through December 2025 (most recent follow-up)
**Context:** meltyhoney_ posted asking for advice ahead of an interview at a dream company. She was anxious about her portfolio: her existing samples were corporate-traditional and didn't reflect creative work, and she'd been out of regular career practice for almost a year after a series of second-round rejections. Mike replied with a multi-comment exchange offering two strategic options. First, spin the existing portfolio by walking through the process and constraints behind the design decisions. Second, build something new and company-specific between then and the interview. He emphasized that the portfolio gets you in the door and the interview gets you hired, and that fit matters more than the strongest possible work sample for an ID-focused role.

**Outcome:** meltyhoney_ built two short training modules from scratch tailored to the company for her interview rounds. She got the job. Per her DM, hiring managers told her she was "leaps and bounds above all other candidates" specifically because of the custom-built work samples. Documented in two follow-up DMs (October 21, 2025 announcing the offer; December 3, 2025 confirming the role was going well).

**Key DM excerpts:**

> Your last message made me tear up and gave me a lot of hope for my future in ID, that I had been questioning heavily up until today. Regardless of whether I get this dream job, I'm changed for the better by your feedback.

> I just wanted to share that I got my dream job! And the hiring managers said I was leaps and bounds above all other candidates because of how above and beyond I went by creating two short modules from scratch for each of the interviews.

> You made a difference in my life and it was always my hope to tell you, with good news attached.

**Notes:** No business relationship. Pure Reddit community contribution producing a documented career outcome. Useful as supporting evidence for ID Atlas's positioning of Field Notes and Reddit as community service rather than lead-gen, and for any future writing about Reddit-based mentorship as a service philosophy.
