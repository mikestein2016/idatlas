## ISRG (Internet Security Research Group)

**Relationship:** Client (custom eLearning development for nonprofit security training)
**Contact:** Carissa
**Channel:** Inbound through Reddit
**Context:** ISRG, the nonprofit behind Let's Encrypt, ran a recurring three-hour face-to-face cybersecurity training over Google Meet with a Google Forms quiz at the end. They wanted something more engaging, more scalable, and trackable through their LMS (Rippling). The training covered cybersecurity fundamentals (HTTPS, certificate authorities, basic security concepts) pitched at a level appropriate for non-technical staff (HR, payroll, operations). It served both compliance requirements and a broader mission-alignment goal: helping every employee at a security-focused nonprofit understand the basics of what the organization does and why it matters.

**Process highlights:**
- Consolidated input from multiple SMEs who held different mental models of the underlying processes, producing a single source-of-truth document. This was itself a valuable internal artifact for ISRG, independent of the resulting eLearning.
- Designed and built an on-demand eLearning module suited to a non-technical audience learning technical concepts.
- Deployed in ISRG's LMS (Rippling) with native progress and score tracking, replacing the prior Google Forms quiz.

**Operational outcomes (no formal KPIs collected):**
- SMEs no longer need to be present in a synchronous Zoom session with all trainees.
- SME bandwidth is reallocated to answering individual questions rather than running live training sessions.
- ISRG now has scalable infrastructure for on-demand compliance training going forward.
- LMS reporting gives ISRG better visibility into where learners struggle.

**Testimonial (received from Carissa, ISRG):**

> Working with Mike was a positive experience! He quickly learned complex subject matter, asked thoughtful questions, and created engaging training modules. He was receptive to feedback, flexible, and highly communicative throughout the process.

**Notes:** Strong source material for a long-form case study or Field Notes post on ID Atlas's nonprofit work, particularly the SME-consolidation step and the synchronous-to-asynchronous training transformation. Notable for proposal credibility: ISRG is the nonprofit behind Let's Encrypt, the certificate authority that secures a substantial share of the public web. Useful logo-credibility lift for proposals targeting other nonprofits, security-adjacent organizations, or compliance-driven L&D programs. Like several other ID Atlas relationships, this engagement came in cold through Reddit.
