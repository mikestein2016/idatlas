# ID Atlas — Blog Post Ideas

A working backlog of post ideas for Field Notes, organized by purpose. Currently leaning marketing-flavored to balance the existing community-service-heavy content mix. Add, reorder, or strike through as posts move from idea to draft to published.

## Operating principle

The line that matters isn't community-vs-marketing, it's pitching-vs-teaching. As long as posts teach something a reader can use, they do marketing work without crossing into salesy territory. The reader draws the inference about hiring on their own.

## Marketing-flavored backlog

### 1. Two-year anniversary retrospective
One-time window. Honest reflection on the apprenticeship-as-agency model: what's worked, what hasn't, what you'd do differently. Marketing happens by demonstrating you're still thinking critically about your own work. Audience: prospects evaluating ID Atlas, plus the ID community at large.

### 2. The anti-bootcamp essay
Position-defining, single-argument long-form. Title direction: "What an Instructional Design Apprenticeship Is, and Why I Stopped Recommending Bootcamps." Evidence base: Cherry, Stephanie, the other graduates. The post that gets shared on Reddit and LinkedIn, brings inquiries from career switchers, and also pulls clients who recognize the same anti-bootcamp instinct in how they hire. Highest-leverage marketing post available right now. Trade-off: takes a public position, which generates both heat and self-selection.

### 3. Buyer's-POV post
Title direction: "How to hire an instructional designer: an honest guide from a vendor." Written for HR, ops, L&D buyers, founders, nonprofit directors. Covers what to ask in an intake call, red flags in proposals, how to scope an RFP, how to tell whether you need an ID or an eLearning developer. Reaches the audience with budget, which the current Field Notes does not.

### 4. AI-in-our-workflow post
Timely, demonstrates ID Atlas is current. Concrete examples of how Claude plus an LMS shell is replacing dedicated authoring tools in production. Lays groundwork for the eventual open-source authoring tool launch. Performs well on LinkedIn.

### 5. Additional case studies (rolling)
Each substantive client engagement becomes a case study post, written as transferable lesson rather than brag. ISRG is the first in queue. Add Texas State serious game (if not already covered in depth), Grow with Trees, and others as appropriate.
